Ada Lovelace: The First Computer Programmer


Born on December 10, 1815, Ada Lovelace, an English Mathematician is known as the first computer programmer for writing an algorithm for a computing machine in the mid-1800s. Lovelace is a role model for many young women especially because she made a mark in the technological world long before computers were a thing, and she managed to do this in a time when women were not supported in interests like these.

Grace Hopper: The First Computer Programmer of MARK I Computer


Born on December 9, 1906, Grace Brewster Murray Hopper was an American computer scientist and United States Navy rear admiral. She was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer. Grace Hopper gave a lot to the world of computer science as she helped develop COBOL; one of the first high-level programming languages and invented the first compiler.

Carol Shaw: The First Women Game Programmer and Designer


In 1978, Carol Shaw was the first woman to program and design a video game, 3D Tic-Tac-Toe for the Atari 2600.

Donna Dubinsky: Developer of the First Personal Digital Assistant


Donna Dubinsky is an American business leader who played an integral role in the development of personal digital assistants serving as CEO of Palm, Inc. and co-founding Handspring with Jeff Hawkins in 1995.

Radia Perlman: Mother of Internet"


Radia Perlman is often referred as the "Mother of Internet" for her her invention of the spanning-tree protocol, which is fundamental to the operation of network bridges, while working for Digital Equipment Corporation.

Smalltalk - 80


Adele Goldberg (born July 22, 1945) is a computer scientist who participated in developing the programming language Smalltalk-80 and various concepts related to object-oriented programming while a researcher at the Xerox Palo Alto Research Center (PARC), in the 1970s.
